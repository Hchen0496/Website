About Me

Hi everyone, my name is Huilin Chen, I'm about to graduate in Fall 2019 with my bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering Technology.
Since young I've always had been into technology by itself, such as the hardware, software, programming, networking and so on. Before I had gotten to love programming one of the things that made me who I am today is the struggles I've been through.

A lot of people that are like me thinks they love every single thing about the advanced of technology, however that is not the case. I started off to hate programming when I first entered my freshmen year of college. This was due to the lack of hands-on experience that left me feeling lost in what I really want to do within my major. Towards the end of my Sophmore year in college when I took a class that requires the use of an embedded system called, "Arduino", I was really able to grasp the fundamental of programming with the language 'C' with hands-on experience compare to just reading and memorizing vocabulary as what I had done in the first year for the language of python. As I continue to take courses that requires programming, such as C++ and Java. I was able to fall in love with programming.

At times, when things get rough I would be on a continuous stress about whether or not I'm fitted for this, but my determination and self-motivation wouldn't allow me to give up.
